# Fortitude — Brand Guidelines

## Positioning

Fortitude is a **consultancy-first, founder-led, discreet security firm** — not a guard supplier, not a corporate agency. The brand sits closer to a private bank or white-glove concierge service than a traditional security company.

**Three words:** Discretion. Intelligence. Precision.

Every design and copy decision should reinforce: *exclusive, understated, trustworthy.*

---

## Target Audience

| Segment                  | Description                                                          |
|--------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Royal Households         | Discreet, long-term protection relationships. Non-negotiable trust.  |
| Ultra-High-Net-Worth     | Complex threat profiles, international travel, lifestyle security.   |
| Family Offices           | Multi-generational risk oversight, asset and personnel protection.   |
| Corporate Executives     | C-suite with elevated exposure, travel risk, executive protection.   |
| VIP Visitors to UAE      | Short-term engagements, local expertise, rapid response.             |

These clients share common requirements:
- Absolute discretion — confidentiality is non-negotiable
- Bespoke solutions — they will not accept an off-the-shelf product
- Founder-led engagement — they are dealing with a person, not a company
- Regulatory compliance — SIRA certification and international standards matter

---

## Tone of Voice

**Confident, understated, authoritative.**

- Write as if addressing a peer, not a prospect
- No corporate jargon, no security industry clichés
- Short sentences. Declarative. No hedging.
- Credentials are stated once, clearly — never repeated for effect
- The tone is that of a senior advisor, not a salesperson

**Do:**
- "Founder-led engagement at every stage."
- "Intelligence-driven. Prevention-first."
- "Bespoke to your threat profile and lifestyle."

**Don't:**
- "World-class security solutions for your peace of mind"
- "Our team of experts delivers cutting-edge protection"
- "We're here for you 24/7" (too generic)

| `--gold`             | `#c9a84c`                  | Primary accent — links, labels, highlights  |
| `--gold-muted`       | `rgba(201,168,76,0.08)`    | Subtle fills (panel headers)                |
| `--gold-border`      | `rgba(201,168,76,0.18)`    | Dividers, card borders                      |
| `--gold-border-mid`  | `rgba(201,168,76,0.30)`    | Button borders, hover states                |
| `--text-primary`     | `#ede9df`                  | Headings, strong text — warm off-white      |
| `--text-secondary`   | `#8d919e`                  | Body copy                                   |
| `--text-muted`       | `#505568`                  | Captions, meta, subtle labels               |

**Gold rationale:** `#c9a84c` is warmer and more muted than standard gold — it reads as aged and refined rather than brash, consistent with the heraldic crest in the logo.

**Off-white rationale:** `#ede9df` has a slight warm undertone that pairs with the gold and avoids the clinical feel of pure white on dark backgrounds.

## Typography

### Headings — Cormorant Garamond
Loaded from Google Fonts. Used by private banks, luxury fashion houses, and high-end editorial print.

| Weight | Usage                        |
|--------|------------------------------|
| 300    | Hero h1, section h2          |
| 400    | Service titles, sub-headings |
| Italic | Available but used sparingly |

**Note:** The hero h1 is set in weight 300, non-italic. Italic was removed at client request for consistency.

### Body — System Font Stack
```
-apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, 'Segoe UI', sans-serif
```
No additional load. Clean contrast against the editorial serif headings.

## Design Principles

1. **Space is luxury.** Cramped layouts signal cheapness. Every section has room to breathe.
2. **No icons.** Typography and layout carry hierarchy. Icons are generic.
3. **No animations.** Transitions on hover only. Nothing moves unless touched.
4. **Copy earns its place.** Every paragraph should justify its existence.
5. **The founder is the brand.** Credentials and biography are the most important content. Design supports them.
6. **Discretion in the design itself.** No bold gradients, no dramatic effects. Confidence through restraint.

---

## What Not to Do

- Do not use bright colours, gradients, or animated hero sections
- Do not use generic stock photography of security guards or earpieces
- Do not use corporate taglines or sales-speak
- Do not highlight headcount, number of operators, or "team size"
- Do not introduce new typefaces — Cormorant Garamond + system sans only
- Do not alter the dark colour palette — it is a deliberate brand signal
